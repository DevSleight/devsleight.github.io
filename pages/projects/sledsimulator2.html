Project Overview

Sled Simulator 2 is the sequel to Sled Simulator, also powered by the Roblox Engine. It was developed enhance the existing codebase but was instead completely re-written to make it easier to introduce new features, such as trading and allowing players to possess multiple items of the same type with varying properties.

Customization

Players have access to a personal garage where they can customize their equipped parts and showcase their sleds to others. The garage features different camera angles depending on the type of part being changed, enhancing the customization experience.

Parts

In Sled Simulator 2, all items have unique IDs instead of being unlockable. This approach facilitates trading, allows for distinct properties for each part, and enables players to own multiple instances of each item. For example, properties can include whether a part is painted or features special effects.

Upgrading sleds

In Sled Simulator 2, upgrades are tied to the player and the map they are currently on, rather than the sled parts themselves. In the first game, many of the added parts were cosmetic, with players retaining only the best upgrades from previous parts. I wanted to maintain this mechanic in the sequel, so now every part is purely cosmetic.

Players seeking a greater challenge can still use earlier upgrades by lowering their "equipped" upgrade level. This allows for a customizable difficulty experience while maintaining the game's progression system.
